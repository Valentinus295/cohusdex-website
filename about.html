Built By Someone Who Lives It

CohusDex didn't start in a boardroom. It started in Migori Market.

🧮

Valentine Owuor

Founder & CEO, CohusDex

I'm an Economics & Statistics student at Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology. But my real education came from watching my mother — a vegetable seller in Migori Market — run her business for 20 years without ever knowing her daily profit.

She's not alone. Across Africa, 500 million informal workers run businesses without basic financial tools. No profit & loss. No cash flow tracking. No credit scores. They're invisible to the economy — and the economy is worse for it.

I built Faidaza for her. And CohusDex for the ecosystem that should serve her.

🎯

Our Mission

Make every informal worker visible to the economy. Give them the tools, data, and access that formal businesses take for granted. Start with a voice. Build to an ecosystem.

🌍

Our Vision

An Africa where no mother sells vegetables for 20 years without knowing her daily profit. Where the informal economy is measured, understood, and served. Where every worker has a CFO.

The Name

CohusDex — Co (together) + husuda (jealousy → skill, in Kiswahili) + dex (index).

Turning the energy of aspiration into an index of economic power. Every worker counted. Every transaction recorded. Every person seen.
