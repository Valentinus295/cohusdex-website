The Founder's Story

My mother is a vegetable seller in Migori Market. Every day for over 20 years, she wakes up at 4am, goes to the wholesale market, buys tomatoes, onions, sukuma wiki — and sells them at her stall. She works 12 hours a day, 6 days a week.

But here's the thing that hit me during my second year studying Economics and Statistics: my mother never knew her daily profit. She had no bookkeeper. No calculator. No system. She just worked and hoped there was something left at the end of the day.

She is not alone. There are 500 million informal workers across Africa — mama mbogas, boda-boda riders, fundis, wavuvi, wakulima — none of whom can answer the most basic question in business: "Did I make money today?"

That question became CohusDex. Not just an app — a mission to give every informal worker in Africa the economic intelligence that the formal sector takes for granted. A voice-first CFO that speaks your language, works offline, and costs nothing.

We call it Faidaza. It means "profit" — but also "benefit," "gain," "advantage." Because knowing your numbers isn't just accounting. It's power.

Mission & Vision

Mission

To make economic intelligence accessible to every informal worker in Africa — starting with a voice they already speak, on a phone they already own, at a price they can afford: free.

Vision

An Africa where every informal worker — from the mama mboga in Migori to the fisherman in Lamu — knows exactly where their business stands. Where the informal economy is not invisible, but illuminated.

The Journey

How we got here.

May — August 2025

KALRO — Non-Ruminant Research Institute

Three-month attachment in Kakamega. Applied statistical methods to agricultural data. Built the foundation: understanding how data transforms decision-making in rural economies. First exposure to the data gap between farmers and policymakers.

2025 — Present

Demulla Microfinance — Field Research

Current internship conducting field research for CohusDex Score. Interviewing microfinance loan officers, analyzing repayment patterns, and building the credit intelligence models that will let MFIs lend confidently to the unbanked.

2025 — Present

UNDP Digital, AI & Innovation Internship

Application #33001 submitted. Position focused on digital innovation for development. If selected: applying global frameworks to CohusDex's mission of digitizing the informal economy.

2024 — Present

CohusDex Founded

Born from a question in a Migori market stall. Named CohusDex — from "co" (together), "husuda" (Swahili: jealousy/envy transformed into motivation), and "dex" (index). A company built to transform the jealousy of seeing others succeed into the skills to succeed yourself.

December 2026

Graduation — Masinde Muliro University

Bachelor of Economics and Statistics. 44 degree units of rigorous quantitative training — now applied to the most under-measured economy on Earth.
